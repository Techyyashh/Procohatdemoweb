What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security. It operates independently of a central authority, like a bank or government. Cryptocurrencies rely on blockchain technology, a distributed ledger enforced by a network of computers. They allow secure online payments and transactions without intermediaries.

Popular cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and others offer unique features such as decentralization, transparency, and programmability, making them revolutionary in the financial and technological world.

Types of Cryptocurrency

Bitcoin

The first and most popular cryptocurrency.

Ethereum

A platform for decentralized applications and smart contracts.

Ripple

Designed for fast and low-cost international payments.

Litecoin

A lightweight alternative to Bitcoin.

Cardano

A blockchain platform for changemakers, innovators, and visionaries.

Dogecoin

Started as a meme, now a widely used cryptocurrency.

Polkadot

A multi-chain network enabling cross-blockchain transfers.

Tether

A stablecoin pegged to the value of fiat currencies.
